about [me]

I’m Paula, a 22 year old graphic design student based in Madrid, passionate about film and visual storytelling. I move between minimal and bold aesthetics to create work with character.

[ROOM 001]

[POSTERS]

A PROVOCATIVE POSTER INSPIRED BY STANLEY KUBRIK´S "THE CLOCKWORK ORANGE" ICONIC SCENCE THROUGH BOLD TYPOGRAPHY AND FRAGMENTED FRAMING. THE INSTENSE RED PALETTE AND VISUAL REPETITION EMPHASIZE AN UNSETTLING GAZE, EXPLORING THE RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN IMAGE, OBSESSION AND CINEMATIC NARRATIVE.

A MINIMALIST BLACK-AND-WHITE ADD THAT USES ABSESNCE AS ITS CENTRAL THEME, FEATURING AN EMPTY CHAIR DRAPED WITH A WHITE SHIRT. THE STRAK COMPOSITION AND SOFT LIGHTNING EVOKE A SENSE OF LOSS AND LINGERING PRESENCE, WHILE THE RESTRAINED TYPOGRAPHY REINFORCES A QUIET, INTROSPECTIVE NARRATIVE ALIGNED WITH CALVIN KLEIN´S UNDERSTATED AESTHETIC.

[ROOM 002]

[BRANDING]

A BOLD REBRANDING CONCEPT FOR MOCO MUSEUM THAT BLENDS CONTEMPORARY TYPOGRAPHY WITH PLAYFUL, FLUID VISUAL ELEMENTS. THE IDENTITY IS BUILT AROUND A VIBRANT PINK PALETTE AND MODULAR APPLICATIONS ACROSS POSTERS, PACKAGING AND MERCHANDISING, CREATING A COHESIVE YET DYNAMIC SYSTEM THAT REFLECTS THE MUSEUM´S EXPERIMENTAL AND MODERN SPIRIT.

[ROOM 003]

[FILM]

PERMANENTE - A SHORT FILM THAT FOLLOWS AGNES, A YOUNG ACTRESS REHEARSING IN FRONT OF A MIRROR THE NIGHT BEFORE AN AUDITION. WHAT BEGINS AS AN INTENSE AND METICULOUS PREPARATION GRADUALLY SPIRALS INTO AN UNEXPECTED AND COMEDIC TURN, REVEALING THE ABSURDITY AND VULNERABILTY BEHIND PERFORMANCE AND SELF-PERCEPTION.

CINE-FILIA - A SHORT FILM THAT EXPLORES THE IDEA OF LOVE AT FIRST SIGHT THROUGH A YOUNG WOMAN´S ENCOUNTER WITH CINEMA. UPON WATCHING MARTIN SCORSESE´S “TAXI DRIVER”, SHE EXPERIENCES AN INMEDIATE AND TRANSFORMATIVE CONNECTION, CAPTURING THE INTENSITY OF FALLING IN LOVE, NOT WITH A PERSON, BUT WITH THE POWER AND ALLURE OF FILM ITSELF.
